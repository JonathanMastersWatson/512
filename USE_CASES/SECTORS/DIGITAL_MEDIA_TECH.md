# Media and Information Systems

## Scope

This document describes media and information systems operating under
the constraints of the 512 Kernel.

This includes journalism, social platforms, recommendation systems,
and synthetic media.

---

## Editorial Moderation

Problem:
Editorial decisions are often enforced through opaque moderation rules.

Kernel Constraint:
Rules governing moderation must be visible and referenceable.

Outcome:
Systems must disclose moderation policies and provide contestable
outcomes or exit.

---

## Algorithmic Ranking

Problem:
Ranking and suppression logic is hidden and unchallengeable.

Kernel Constraint:
No hidden rules governing interaction.

Outcome:
Ranking logic must be declared, or users must be able to exit or
export without penalty.

---

## Synthetic and AI-Generated Media

Problem:
Generated content is presented as human or neutral.

Kernel Constraint:
No fraud or misrepresentation.

Outcome:
Systems must disclose when content is synthetic or agent-generated.

---

## Copyright and Takedowns

Problem:
Silent or asymmetric takedowns.

Kernel Constraint:
Contracts must be explicit and equally enforceable.

Outcome:
Takedowns must reference governing rules and allow contest or exit.

---

## Source Protection Systems

Problem:
Trust is asserted without accountability.

Kernel Constraint:
Fail-open without exposing protected parties.

Outcome:
Rule paths are disclosed without revealing source identities.
