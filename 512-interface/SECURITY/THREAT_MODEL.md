# Threat Model for 512

This document describes the threat boundaries of 512.

It is descriptive, not exhaustive.

---

## Threats Addressed by 512

512 is designed to reduce or eliminate:

- silent modification of governing rules
- hidden or undisclosed authorities
- retroactive changes to constraints
- asymmetric rule visibility between participants
- unverifiable claims of legitimacy

These threats are addressed through immutability, hashing, and public anchoring.

---

## Threats Not Addressed by 512

512 does not protect against:

- physical coercion
- legal compulsion
- cartel behavior
- social or economic pressure
- censorship by force
- coordinated off-ledger collusion

512 assumes adversarial environments.

---

## Trust Assumptions

512 assumes:

- cryptographic primitives function as specified
- public ledgers provide irreversible ordering
- participants can independently verify hashes
- adoption is voluntary

512 does not assume honesty, goodwill, or alignment.

---

## Failure Modes

512 fails when:

- participants ignore verification
- systems claim compliance without anchoring
- users defer trust to authority instead of hashes

These failures are observable, not silent.

---

## Summary

512 does not eliminate power.

512 removes hidden power.
