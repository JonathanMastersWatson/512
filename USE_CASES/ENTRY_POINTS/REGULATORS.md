# 512 for Regulators

512 is an audit primitive.

It does not replace regulation.

---

## Purpose

512 enables verification of:

- disclosed rules
- rule immutability
- absence of hidden authority

It does not mandate outcomes.

---

## What 512 Provides

- transparent constraint disclosure
- verifiable governance claims
- irreversible historical anchoring

---

## What 512 Does Not Do

512 does not:

- enforce compliance
- override law
- assign liability
- adjudicate disputes

---

## Regulatory Use

Regulators may:

- require disclosure of hashes
- verify consistency over time
- compare claimed rules to anchored rules

---

## Summary

512 reduces information asymmetry.

It does not regulate behavior.
